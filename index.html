Kuppons

UI/UX Design

I spoke with my client to discuss an idea for an eCommerce website that focused promoting Amazon products with a rebate incentive. The timeline for this project was very tight and as a person with little tech background the requirements for the project were very vague. The main goals of my client’s site was to gain the trust of users, convert, and also have returning users. My main concerns for this project was my inexperience with rebate based commerce and also the vague description of the project which would make it difficult to pinpoint the meaning of a successful design.

I decided to take on this project since it would be my opportunity to work with the client and to build a list of requirements for the project. I would then need to apply my own skills from working in eCommerce and learn about building a trusting relationship with an interface of a site that will be completely foreign to a user.


Research and Planning

I carried out a competitive and heuristic analysis to gain an understanding of the different mental models present in different types of users of eCommerce platforms. Going through different sites, I noticed that they all had their own efficient way of organizing their information tailored to their user's goals. 


Persona Creation

To gather information regarding the mental model of the target users I carried out surveys and interviews that uncover user's preferences, attitudes, and feelings towards certain features, events, and situations. The surveys consisted of questions that rated the the user's reactions and attitudes towards certain features and interactions. The interview questions consisted of open ended questions to dig deeper into the users mental model and uncover potential areas for innovation. I used the information derived from surveys and interviews to create three personas that embodied three main user goals that my design will revolve around.


Personas:

Andrea


Goals


  • Find new trending items
  • Look for new accessories
  • Plan future outfits
  • Change wardrobe with the season

Description

This user is a sporadic shopper who is in no rush. She is looking to replenishing her current stock of items and also open to trying the latest products. She often window shops and takes her time when shopping for gifts and for herself. She takes pleasure in looking through all of her choices before making her final decision. All of the marketing emails she receives are always read and marked.

Jen


Goals


  • Pay the lowest price
  • Find great deals
  • Find complementary items that would work well together
  • Buy gifts early for their friends

Description

The bargain hunter is a shopper on a mission to find the best deal she can and  she does all of her research to ensure she is getting the most bang for her buck. She is constantly comparing new items she comes across to the items she has seen before. A transparent return policy is important since she does not want to waste time and money on something she might dislike

Jake


Goals


  • Save time and money
  • Only shops when needs something specific
  • Find item as soon as possible
  • Easy payment method

Description

His focus is to find the item he is looking for purchase it and then get back to doing his homework. He is very busy and has little patience for browsing through a website. This user is a fan of creating lists filling out planners and getting his tasks done. He only shops when he needs something specific and has one favorite retailer.


Design Process


My iterative design process starts with the creation a site map and user flows to ensure that all of the features of the site are accessible through the appropriate navigation. of wire frames that I occasionally test with my peers to ensure a cohesive and intuitive interface following a lean design method. My next step is to create low fidelity prototypes that bring the interactions to life and produce a minimum viable product for initial validation testing.


Prototyping

My first steps in prototyping include the creation of a site map to ensure that every feature of the interface is meaningful and allows users to access essential features of the site. Through site map creation I am able to visualize the user flow and pin point common drop off points that adversely affect the user experience. For the final unmoderated remote user testing I wanted to ensure that the interface was intuitive and allowed users to complete their goals with minimal pain points and efficiency. I compiled a list of tasks and follow up questions to ensure that the tasks were completed correctly. If the tasks were completed correctly, there would be a specific code revealed that would allow me to ensure the predicted path was taken by the user in order to complete the task.


Reflection

While the direction of this project was initially vague from the instruction of my client, I was able to pin point certain features that would increase conversion on a site that would be completely new to the user. The most important goal of this project is to create an experience that is familiar and one that will encourage users to trust the site and convert. This project taught me the importance of working with lean UX in mind since I had close deadline.Through completing this project I was able to better understand the elements of flat UI and also work with creating my own scalable pixel perfect icons.​

Tender

UX Research & Design

I worked in a team of four people creating a iPad based POS system that would provide the best experience for the user and allow them to confidently and efficiently enter transactions. This extensive process incorporated the various elements of user centered design such as user testing, competitive analysis, and heuristic analysis.


Research

Our first step was identifying key features that users needed to reach their main goals with our product. I was responsible for conducting user interviews and surveys that helped us identify these features. Then a card sorting exercise was conducted to better understand the user's informational mind set. My group then conducted an A/B testing to track user's reactions and behaviours towards different visual design aspects. In addition, my team conducted a competitive analysis in order to evaluate our products functionality in comparison with current POS systems out on the market.


Design

I worked on many aspects in the iterative design process including the selection of a muted color scheme and the placement of app features. We collaborated and had multiple brainstorming sessions before creating mock ups for us to defend our own ideas. After collaborating and meeting with our manager, we were able to recieve the approval to create a high fidelity prototype with invision.



User Testing

I did some independent research before assisting in the usability process which included participating in user testing sessions hosted by Depaul Human Computer Interaction students. The Depaul user testing process served as a model for our own. We noted the format of their user testing script and incorporated our own ideas to make it more effective for our own case. Our team then went on to recruit target users as well as fringe cases to test our prototype.

Pinterest

Full-Stack Development

M.E.A.N (MongoDB, Express, Angular 2, Node.js)


My team worked on a project that is modeled after the pinterest social media site.I was in charge of putting together the front end styling of this project and I wanted to challenge my front-end development skills by structuring the client side to be as close to the pinterest website as possible using CSS functions.


Planning

The first step in planning was going through the pinterest site and identifying the interactions that would need to be created to give our product the same look and feel of the pinterest website. After listing all of the features, my team then cut down the list to a shorter list essential to the MVP.

Before starting any work on the project my team used post-it note planning to identify blockers and find the most effective progression of work. We then each chose to take over several user stories that we would all build from database to the client side. We then proceeded to prioritize features of the project using the MoSCoW method and build pieces of the project that had the least number of blockers.


My User Stories

In addition to working on the front end styling I was responsible for the board creation, exploring, and the profile page user stories. I recognized that everyone on of my stories had a blocker that was dependent on the creation of the user and also the pins created by said users. I communicated to my team that I would initially work the front end styling until the models that I needed were created and I would be able to grab pins to place into the boards.


Daily Workflow

Each day my team conducted stand-ups where we talked about our progress into our sections of the project and also if we needed any help from one another. We also had end of day meetings where we talked about the goals we had met and our expectations for the next day to stay on track with our deadline.

Retrospective


Positives

The team worked very well with communication with the aid of daily stand ups that helped each of us gain perspective and an understanding of where our project was realistically heading. Our team was also able to do a lot of our own research with discovering solutions to problems that were not covered in our original MEAN stack documentation.


Deltas

There was not a very clear division of labor since the MEAN stack was more complicated than we initially had planned. Our team ran into multiple errors that required our instructors assistance in order to resolve the problem. In the middle of our project, the team pushed their work on to github which caused the web to crash in each of our machines. We could not figure out the issue until an instructor got involved and identified that the dist file was not being ignored in our git repository.

There were also multiple times where my work depended on my team mate's work where they had taken longer to complete where I stepped in and completed their part for them. I also felt that we could have been more efficient in our planning where we would not have needed to work on essential features till the last minute before our project was being presented.


Challenges


Exploration Page

The explore page was a challenge because of the different calls needed to filter through the list of pin object in the database. My initial approach was to use different calls that would return a list of pins dependent on the conditional statements of the method. I was able to pull pins from the database using an HTTP request and then traversed through the data using an ng for in loop. As for as styling I was able to build a display using CSS columns to mimic the display on pinterest. Each pin has the functionality of being added onto a board. I then needed to create a way to filter through the pins. Instead of a completely new http request I wrote html code that reloaded each component with an ng conditional statement to only display pins related to the category being highlighted.


Adding Pins to Boards

I had to build the functionality of being able to add pins to the user created boards. My initial approach was to build a light box interaction using javascript but this was not possible since I had limited time and the way angular works is that the light box would have had to be a completely new component all together. With limited time I looked through my resources and came across documentation on modals. I was able to build a modal with an ID tag that is linked to an anchor tag. I then added css styling in order to darken the background to simulate a light box interaction. I was then faced with the challenge of building interactions in the "light box" without nesting the anchor tags. Each interaction needed to be an input which sent information to the server and carried out the logic that added the pin into the board.

WAG Dog

Full-Stack Development

C# | ASP.NET


My team worked on a project modeled after match.com which is a popular dating site. My team decided to go the creative route and tackle the problem from a different angle. We decided to build a product called "Who's A Good Dog" (WAG Dog) for the niche dog owners that create social media profiles for their dogs. The challenge of converting the preferences of human users on match.com to the dog preferences in WAG dog was appealing and we enjoyed ourselves creating this app.


Planning

The first stages of app development involved an analysis of the match.com site and uncovering the features needed for users to achieve their goals. The team collectively went into match.com, created profiles, and then compared notes on different functionalities of the site. As a team we were able to list all of the features on a white board and point out the essential features for our MVP. Keeping the MVP in mind, we were able to convert the features into full user stories that represented a user task that each of us would be building from database all the way to the client-facing interface.


My User Stories

My section of the project included developing the view of your own and other's profiles, messaging and sending winks, viewing and filtering results, and finally, I was responsible for the design and development of the navigation. In order to effectively carry out my tasks, I planned out a journey map which consisted of multiple touch points the user would come accross while using our app. By doing so, I was able to identify blockers in my work flow which then led me to work on the navigation first since this task had no dependency on any other feature of the site.


Building Models


My team recognized that the relationships between preferences and the users would be very complicated which is what led us to carefully plan out our models before hand. We were able to analyze different join tables necessary for the site's functionality which allowed us to focus on our own tasks once our data base was constructed. Towards the end of the project my team member identified a crucial issue with the models but we were quickly able to resolve the problem through effective planning and communication.


Project Scope

We conducted daily stand-ups in order for us to maintain an open line of communication. While building on our own sections of the app , we all realized that a lot of our user stories had touch points that were dependent on one another's work. For example, I would not be able to work on the sending and recieving of messages until the user entities were created. Our group decided to hold three meetings a day which would allow us to keep track of each other's progress. This proved very useful since it allowed us to align our work flows with one another which allowed us to be more productive and also keep our merge-conflicts to a minimum.


Styling

One of my team members decided to build a style guide with basic functions and styles that we as a team decided to follow. I was able to experience developing visual elements that were not part of my own vision. I found that following a style guide allowed me to be more objective in the development of my own featues. While I was not the main stylist of the site I was able to assist by applying my CSS skills to the search pages, search filtering, messaging, and the profile pages.


App Demo

I have included a walkthrough of our app in a video below since the deployment through heroku is still in progress. I am walking through the log in / registration process viewing matches, sorting, and sending messages. Our users are also able to post pictures, update their profile information, and block other users.



Retrospective


Positives

My team did a great job of effective planning where we only had to re structure our models a few times. With all of the different relationships required from the database we were all diligent in our planning and labeled foreign keys efficiently which led to a smooth integreation of our pieces. Eventhough we needed to overlap and complete each other's work we were able to do so efficiently through effective and constant communication. Since this is our first strongly typed language, my group did an excellent job maintaining the integrity of our app by synchronized git pushing and also conflict management. We managed to resolve each of our issues independently and make good use of our time.


Deltas

With this project I decided to not take on the breadth of the styling work which was assigned to my other two team members but towards the end of the project the styling was passed on to me. I felt that as a designer I should have communicated my process to them more efficiently so that it would have been easier to build a consistent style guide. I found myself creating multple classes to manipulate certain elements that should have been unified.


Challenges


Filtering Users

Since I was in charge of the display of all users on the home page, I took on the interaction of ebing able to filter users. I initally reloaded the page with each request. I felt that this was not a smooth interaction for the user so I built separate HTML pages for each query that would display using jQuery.


Messaging/h4>

My messaging interaction came as a struggle since I am not the best with databases. I needed to create a table for messages which then needed to be pulled into the client side while being able to view the message and the sender. I feel that I could have been more efficient in my code in reducing the amount of nested loops in order to achieve the end result.
